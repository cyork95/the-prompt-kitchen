The honest truth about AI model differences

Here's what most AI comparison articles don't say: for everyday tasks, the differences between the major AI tools are smaller than the differences between a good prompt and a bad prompt.

Writing a well-briefed, specific prompt into a free AI tool will almost always beat a vague, generic prompt into the most expensive model. The recipes in this kitchen matter more than the appliance you cook them in.

That said, the tools genuinely do have different strengths. Some are better at nuanced writing. Some are faster and cheaper. Some are connected to the internet and can look things up. Some are already built into software you already use. Knowing this helps you pick the right tool for the right job.

💡

Our honest recommendation for beginners

Start with whatever has a free tier and is easiest for you to access. Claude, ChatGPT, and Grok all have solid free options — Grok at grok.com in particular requires no subscription at all. Try one for a week on real tasks and see how it feels. You can always switch.

All of these tools have free tiers you can start with right now — no credit card required. Copy a prompt from the Recipe Book, then paste it into any of these to try it out.

Claude (Anthropic)

🧠

Claude

Made by Anthropic · claude.ai · Free & paid tiers

Claude is widely considered to produce some of the most natural, well-reasoned prose of any AI model. It's thoughtful, thorough, and honest about what it doesn't know. It's particularly good at long documents, nuanced analysis, and writing that doesn't sound robotic.

Best at:

Long-form writing Nuanced analysis Following complex instructions Summarizing documents Code explanation Honest, balanced answers

Worth knowing:

No image generation Free tier has daily message limits Web browsing varies by plan

Free tier: Yes — limited daily messages, access to the Claude Sonnet model. Generous enough for everyday use.

Paid (Claude Pro): ~$20/month. More messages, access to more powerful models, priority during busy periods.

Which Claude model should I use?

Anthropic offers several Claude models. For everyday use, Claude Sonnet (available on the free tier) is excellent. Claude Opus is their most capable model, available on paid plans — use it for complex research, long documents, or tasks where quality really matters. Claude Haiku is faster and cheaper, good for quick tasks where speed matters more than depth.

ChatGPT / GPT-4o (OpenAI)

💬

ChatGPT

Made by OpenAI · chat.openai.com · Free & paid tiers

ChatGPT is the AI most people have heard of — and for good reason. It was the first broadly-available AI chat tool, which means it has the largest community, the most tutorials, and the widest ecosystem of integrations. GPT-4o (their current flagship) is fast, capable, and excellent for a wide range of tasks.

Best at:

General-purpose tasks Code and debugging Web browsing (paid) Image generation via DALL-E Custom GPTs / plugins Voice mode

Worth knowing:

Free tier uses older model Can be overconfident Usage caps on free

Free tier: Yes — access to GPT-4o Mini (capable but less powerful). Limited access to GPT-4o with usage caps.

Paid (ChatGPT Plus): ~$20/month. Full GPT-4o access, web browsing, DALL-E image generation, custom GPTs.

Gemini (Google)

♊

Gemini

Made by Google · gemini.google.com · Free & paid tiers

Gemini is Google's AI, and its biggest advantage is its deep integration with Google's ecosystem — Search, Gmail, Docs, Maps, YouTube. If you're a Google Workspace user, Gemini is worth taking seriously. It's also one of the best models for tasks requiring up-to-date information, since it has real-time web access built in.

Best at:

Real-time web access Google Workspace integration Multimodal (images, audio, video) Research with citations Very long documents

Worth knowing:

Creative writing lags behind Claude Can feel more corporate in tone

Free tier: Yes — Gemini 1.5 Flash is available free. Very generous limits for everyday use.

Paid (Gemini Advanced): ~$20/month (often bundled with Google One). Unlocks Gemini 1.5 Pro and deeper Workspace integration.

Grok (xAI)

𝕏

Grok

Made by xAI · grok.com · Free tier available · Paid via SuperGrok or X Premium+

Grok is xAI's AI assistant, available as a free standalone app at grok.com — no X account required. It's also built into X (formerly Twitter) with real-time access to posts and trending topics. Grok 3 (their latest) is competitive with the top models on reasoning and technical tasks, and the free tier at grok.com is one of the more generous options available right now.

Best at:

Real-time X/Twitter data Current events and trends Casual, direct communication Technical tasks (Grok 3) Image generation (Aurora)

Worth knowing:

Tone can be too casual for professional use Best features require X Premium

Free tier: Yes — grok.com offers a free account with access to Grok models, no X/Twitter account required. A genuinely usable free option for general AI tasks.

Paid (SuperGrok): ~$30/month standalone, or included with X Premium+ (~$22/month). Unlocks higher usage limits, image generation via Aurora, and deeper X integration.

⚠️

Free tier token limits on long prompts

Grok's free tier has per-request token limits that can block longer, context-heavy prompts. If you're sending a detailed research brief — particularly one that includes background context, multi-part requirements, or a long conversation history — you may hit a "too many tokens" error on the free tier before getting a response. SuperGrok removes or significantly raises these limits. For complex multi-constraint prompts, try shortening your request or upgrading if you hit the ceiling regularly.

Microsoft Copilot

🪟

Microsoft Copilot

Made by Microsoft · copilot.microsoft.com · Often free with Windows/M365

Microsoft Copilot is powered by OpenAI's models (GPT-4o) but wrapped in Microsoft's ecosystem. If you use Windows 11, Microsoft 365 (Word, Excel, Outlook, Teams), or Edge, Copilot is right there — often already included in your plan. For office workers, this is frequently the most practical starting point because there's zero setup.

Best at:

Microsoft 365 integration Built into Windows 11 Web-connected answers Office document help Image generation via Designer

Worth knowing:

Not as configurable as standalone models Best features require M365 subscription

Free tier: Yes — Copilot is free at copilot.microsoft.com and built into Windows 11. Copilot Pro is ~$20/month for deeper integration.

Work accounts: Many businesses already have Copilot included in their Microsoft 365 licenses. Check with your IT team.

Perplexity AI

🔍

Perplexity

Made by Perplexity AI · perplexity.ai · Free & paid tiers

Perplexity is a research-first AI that answers questions with cited sources from the live web. Think of it less as a chatbot and more as an intelligent search engine that actually reads the pages for you — and then gives you a concise answer with links to verify. Every response includes references so you can dig deeper or fact-check.

If your main use case is looking things up, researching topics, or staying current on events, Perplexity is often more useful than Claude or ChatGPT for that specific task.

Best at:

Research with cited sources Current events & news Fact-checking Comparative research Always up to date

Worth knowing:

Less conversational than Claude/ChatGPT Not ideal for writing or creative tasks

Free tier: Yes — generous daily usage, web search always included free.

Paid (Pro): ~$20/month. More powerful underlying models (Claude, GPT-4o), file uploads, image generation.

Meta AI

🌐

Meta AI

Made by Meta · meta.ai · Built into WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook · Completely free

Meta AI is completely free and already lives inside apps billions of people use every day. If you use WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, or Messenger, you can access it right now — just type @Meta AI in any conversation. No new account, no subscription, no setup. It's powered by Meta's Llama models, which are competitive with the major commercial models for everyday tasks.

Best at:

Completely free, no limits Accessible via apps you already use Image generation (Imagine feature) Quick everyday questions No extra account needed

Worth knowing:

Less capable than Claude/GPT-4o for complex tasks Privacy: Meta already has your data

Free tier: Yes — completely free, no paid tier currently available.

Best entry point: If you already use WhatsApp, just type @Meta AI in any chat. Or visit meta.ai directly.

DeepSeek

🐋

DeepSeek

Made by DeepSeek (China) · chat.deepseek.com · Generous free tier

DeepSeek surprised the AI world in early 2025 by releasing models that rival GPT-4o-level performance at a fraction of the cost — and with a genuinely free chat interface. It's particularly strong at coding, mathematics, and logical reasoning. The DeepSeek-R1 "thinking" model shows its work step by step for complex problems, similar to OpenAI's o1.

Best at:

Coding & debugging Mathematics & logic Step-by-step reasoning Long document analysis Very generous free tier

Worth knowing:

Chinese company — consider data privacy for sensitive work Can be slow during US peak hours Less polished at creative writing than Claude

Free tier: Yes — chat.deepseek.com is free with generous usage limits.

Paid: No paid chat tier. API access available for developers.

DeepSeek-V3 vs. DeepSeek-R1 — which to use?

DeepSeek-V3 is the standard conversational model — fast, capable, good for most tasks including writing, Q&A, and coding.

DeepSeek-R1 is the "thinking" model — it reasons through problems step by step before answering. Slower, but noticeably better for math, logic puzzles, and complex coding tasks where getting the right answer matters more than speed.

LumoAI (Proton)

🔒

LumoAI

Made by Proton · proton.me/ai · Privacy-first · Free with Proton account

LumoAI is Proton's AI assistant — built by the same team behind ProtonMail and ProtonVPN. The defining feature is privacy: your conversations are end-to-end encrypted and not used to train models. If you already use Proton for email, calendar, or storage, LumoAI is built right into the same ecosystem.

What makes it different from other free AI tools isn't the raw model performance — it's the context. LumoAI can draw on your Proton Mail, Calendar, and Drive to give you personalized, context-aware answers. It knows who you are because it has access to your data, not because you explained it.

Best at:

Privacy — E2E encrypted conversations Personal context from Proton ecosystem Email drafting & summarizing Calendar & task integration Free with any Proton account

Worth knowing:

Smaller model — less capable on complex reasoning than Claude or GPT-4o Best value if you already use Proton apps Less useful as a standalone tool without Proton context

Free tier: Yes — available with a free Proton account at proton.me. Full features included on paid Proton plans.

Paid: Proton Unlimited (~$10/month) unlocks more storage, custom domains, and expanded LumoAI usage alongside the full Proton suite.

💡

Who LumoAI is for

If privacy is a priority — for sensitive work, legal, medical, or personal conversations — LumoAI is the only mainstream AI assistant where conversations are end-to-end encrypted by default. For general everyday tasks, other free tools may have more raw capability, but none of them offer the same privacy guarantee.

Mistral Le Chat

🌬️

Mistral Le Chat

Made in France by Mistral AI · chat.mistral.ai · Free tier available

Mistral AI is Europe's leading AI company, founded in Paris in 2023. Le Chat ("The Cat") is their consumer chat interface — fast, capable, and genuinely competitive with GPT-4o for most everyday tasks. Mistral's models are particularly strong at following structured instructions precisely, multilingual tasks, and coding.

What sets Mistral apart from US counterparts is its open-weights approach — many of their models are freely downloadable and can be run locally. Le Chat itself is the polished hosted version, but the underlying models power a wide range of third-party apps and local deployments.

Best at:

Structured instruction following Multilingual (especially European languages) Coding & technical tasks Open-weights models available GDPR-compliant, EU-based Fast responses

Worth knowing:

Less well-known than US models — fewer tutorials and community resources Image generation not built in (text-only by default) Smaller ecosystem than OpenAI or Google

Free tier: Yes — chat.mistral.ai is free with generous usage. Includes access to Mistral Small and Medium models.

Paid: Mistral Pro (~$14.99/month) unlocks Mistral Large, their most capable model, plus higher usage limits. API access available for developers at competitive rates.

Mistral model lineup — which to use?

Mistral Small — fast and efficient, free on Le Chat. Great for quick tasks, summarising, drafting.

Mistral Medium — better reasoning and instruction following. Available free on Le Chat.

Mistral Large — their flagship model, competitive with GPT-4o. Requires paid plan or API.

Mixtral (open-weights) — a mixture-of-experts model you can download and run locally for free. Popular with developers who want no usage limits and full control.

💡

Why Mistral is worth trying

If data privacy and European regulation compliance matter to your work — GDPR, EU AI Act, healthcare, legal — Mistral is the strongest option. It's also worth trying as a Claude/ChatGPT alternative for structured tasks: it tends to follow formats and output templates very precisely, which makes it useful when you need clean, consistent output.

Poe (Quora)

🐾

Poe

Made by Quora · poe.com · Free tier with daily points · Access to many AI models in one place

Poe is Quora's AI platform — and its unique value is that it's not a single AI model, it's an aggregator. One account, one interface, and you can talk to Claude, ChatGPT (GPT-4o), Gemini, Llama, Mistral, and dozens of other models without separate subscriptions or logins. It also has its own "Assistant" — a lightweight default bot for quick everyday tasks.

The free tier gives you a daily allotment of points. Lightweight models (like the Assistant or Llama) cost fewer points; premium models (like Claude Opus or GPT-4o) cost more. For the technique of running the same prompt across multiple AIs and comparing results — the core skill this site teaches — Poe is the most efficient single tool for doing that.

Best at:

Access to Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Llama, Mistral in one place Compare multiple AI responses side by side No separate accounts needed per model Free daily access to many models Custom bot creation

Worth knowing:

Point system can run out quickly on premium models No persistent memory across sessions by default Free Assistant is less capable than the premium models it hosts Quora privacy policy applies — not designed for sensitive work

Free tier: Yes — poe.com gives daily points for free. Lightweight models replenish faster; premium models (GPT-4o, Claude Opus) burn through points quickly.

Paid: ~$19.99/month for unlimited access to all models including premium tiers.

💡

The best use of Poe

Poe is ideal for the multi-AI comparison technique: run the same prompt on Claude, then GPT-4o, then Gemini, all without switching tabs or logging in and out. Where the models agree, you can be more confident. Where they diverge, you know what to dig into. It's the most efficient way to practice the synthesis approach described throughout the Tasting Menu examples.

You.com

🔍

You.com

you.com · Free tier available · AI-powered search with agentic research workflows

You.com is an AI-powered search engine that goes further than summarising web results — it can run multi-step agentic "Workflows" that search, compare, and synthesise information across dozens of sources in a single query. Think of it as a research assistant that does the legwork of opening multiple tabs and reading them for you, then presents a structured report with all sources cited.

For research-heavy tasks — product comparisons, fact-finding, market research, competitive analysis — You.com often returns more comprehensive results than conversational AI tools, because it's actively querying the live web rather than drawing on training data alone.

Best at:

Agentic research workflows (multi-step searches) Product comparison across multiple retailers Live web results with cited sources Comprehensive coverage — surfaces more results than most Free tier with no account required for basic search

Worth knowing:

Output can be overwhelming — many results to sort through Less useful for creative or conversational tasks Agentic workflows can take longer than simple chat Quality varies — more results doesn't always mean better results

Free tier: Yes — you.com is free for basic search and research. YouChat (conversational mode) is also available free.

Paid: You.com Pro (~$15/month) unlocks more powerful AI models, priority access to agentic features, and higher usage limits.

💡

When to use You.com vs. a conversational AI

Use You.com when you need breadth and sources — shopping comparisons, researching a topic across multiple sites, finding current prices or availability. Use Claude or ChatGPT when you need reasoning and synthesis — making sense of what you've found, drafting, explaining, or deciding. The best research workflows often use both: You.com to gather the raw material, a conversational AI to help you process it.

DuckDuckGo Duck.AI

🦆

DuckDuckGo Duck.AI

duck.ai · No account required · Privacy-first

DuckDuckGo's Duck.AI is the most private way to use a large language model — no sign-up, no tracking, no data stored, no conversation history. You open the page, type, and leave. Nothing is tied to your identity.

Under the hood, Duck.AI lets you switch between several models in the same interface: Llama, Claude 3 Haiku, Mixtral, and GPT-4o mini. It's not the most powerful option in any of those categories, but for quick questions where you don't want to create an account or have your queries linked to a profile, it's hard to beat.

Available models

  • Llama 4 Scout (Meta) — fast, good for general Q&A. Note: Meta has announced this model is being retired — check duck.ai for the current lineup.
  • Claude 3 Haiku (Anthropic) — fast and concise, good for writing and editing.
  • Mixtral 8x7B (Mistral) — strong for structured tasks and multilingual content.
  • GPT-4o mini (OpenAI) — solid general-purpose model at small size.
  • o4-mini (OpenAI) — reasoning-oriented, good for logic and math.
⚠️

Llama 4 Scout is being retired

Meta has announced Llama 4 Scout will be leaving Duck.AI. The available model list on duck.ai changes over time — always check the model selector when you visit to see what's currently offered.

Free tier

Duck.AI is completely free. No account, no credits, no sign-up. Daily usage limits apply, but for occasional use you'll rarely hit them. There is no paid plan — it's a free feature of DuckDuckGo.

Strengths

  • No account needed
  • Zero tracking / no chat history
  • Multiple models in one UI
  • Completely free
  • Good for privacy-sensitive queries

Limitations

  • No persistent memory or history
  • No image generation
  • No file uploads
  • Model lineup changes without notice
  • Smaller context window than full-tier models
💡

When Duck.AI is the right tool

Use Duck.AI when you want a quick answer and don't want to log into anything. It's ideal for one-off questions, privacy-sensitive queries (medical, legal, financial research), or when you're on a shared device. For complex multi-turn tasks, you'll want an account-based tool that keeps context across messages.

Access at: duck.ai or click "AI Chat" from any DuckDuckGo search result page.

Qwen Studio (Alibaba)

🌐

Qwen Studio

chat.qwen.ai · Alibaba Cloud · Free tier available

Qwen is Alibaba's AI assistant, built on the Qwen3 model family. It's one of the most capable publicly accessible models for structured tasks — especially strong at research summaries, comparison tables, and technical writing. The flagship Qwen3-235B-A22B is a mixture-of-experts model that rivals the top Western models on benchmarks, and the open-weights versions can be self-hosted.

Qwen Studio stands out for the depth and structure of its responses. For research-heavy prompts, it tends to return organised tables, cited sources, and actionable summaries rather than plain prose — closer to a structured report than a chat reply.

Model lineup

  • Qwen3-235B-A22B — flagship mixture-of-experts model, highest capability, available on API and some interfaces.
  • Qwen3-Plus — strong balance of speed and capability; available on the free tier at chat.qwen.ai.
  • Qwen3-Turbo — fastest, best for quick tasks; available on the free tier.
  • Qwen3-32B / 72B — open-weights versions available for self-hosting via Hugging Face or Ollama.

Thinking mode

Like DeepSeek-R1, Qwen3 supports a thinking mode that shows its chain-of-reasoning before giving an answer. Enable it for math, logic, and complex research tasks — it trades speed for noticeably better accuracy on hard problems.

Free tier

Free access to Qwen3-Plus and Qwen3-Turbo at chat.qwen.ai. No credit card required. Daily usage limits apply but are generous for individual use. The open-weights models are free to run locally — no account needed at all.

Strengths

  • Structured, table-heavy responses
  • Source citations included
  • Strong at coding and math
  • Multilingual (especially Chinese)
  • Thinking mode for hard problems
  • Open weights available for self-hosting

Limitations

  • Chinese company — data sovereignty concerns similar to DeepSeek
  • Structured output can over-format simple questions
  • Interface less polished than Claude or ChatGPT
  • Web search results may lag behind Perplexity / You.com
💡

When Qwen is the right tool

Use Qwen when you need a structured research output — comparison tables, referenced summaries, technical breakdowns. Its default formatting is closer to a professional report than a casual chat reply. For quick conversational tasks or creative writing, Claude or ChatGPT will feel more natural. For complex logic and math, enable thinking mode.

Hugging Chat (HuggingFace)

🤗

Hugging Chat

huggingface.co/chat · Open-source model hub · Free

Hugging Chat is HuggingFace's interface for running open-source language models directly in your browser. Unlike most AI tools that lock you into one proprietary model, Hugging Chat lets you switch between many different open-source models — Llama, Mistral, Qwen, Command R, and others — all in the same chat interface. It's the best single destination for exploring the open-source AI ecosystem without self-hosting anything.

Hugging Chat Omni refers to their multimodal capability, allowing some models to accept image inputs alongside text — useful for image analysis, document reading, and visual question-answering tasks.

Available models (rotating)

The available model list changes as HuggingFace updates the platform. Common options include Meta Llama 3.3 70B, Qwen2.5 72B, Mistral 7B, Command R+, and various specialised models. Check huggingface.co/chat for the current lineup.

Free tier

Hugging Chat is free and requires no account for basic use. Creating a free HuggingFace account unlocks conversation history and higher rate limits. Rate limits can be aggressive on popular models — if you hit a limit, try switching to a less-loaded model or coming back later.

⚠️

Rate limits and blank responses

Hugging Chat can return blank responses or error silently when it hits capacity limits — especially on larger models during peak hours. If you get no response, try refreshing, switching models, or returning later. Creating a free account typically gives you more reliable access than anonymous use.

Strengths

  • Access many open-source models in one place
  • Multimodal via Omni (image + text)
  • No proprietary lock-in
  • Free, no credit card
  • Open-source transparency

Limitations

  • Aggressive rate limits on popular models
  • Can return blank responses at capacity
  • Model availability changes without notice
  • No built-in web search
  • Quality varies widely by model chosen
💡

When Hugging Chat is the right tool

Use Hugging Chat when you want to experiment with open-source models without self-hosting, compare how different model families respond to the same prompt, or use multimodal Omni features for free. It's also a good fallback when proprietary tools are down or you've hit your daily limits elsewhere.

Access at: huggingface.co/chat

Kimi (Moonshot AI)

🌙

Kimi

kimi.ai · Moonshot AI · Free tier available

Kimi is Moonshot AI's flagship assistant, built on the Kimi K2 model — a 1-trillion-parameter mixture-of-experts model with 32 billion active parameters at inference time. It's designed for long-context tasks and agentic research workflows, with a context window up to 1 million tokens (one of the largest available). Kimi excels at tasks that require reading and synthesising very long documents — contracts, research papers, extensive codebases.

The K2 series is optimised for coding and agentic tasks. Kimi tends to produce comprehensive, structured responses with ranked summaries and comparison tables — similar in style to Qwen but from a different Chinese AI lab.

Model lineup

  • Kimi K2 — flagship model, 1T parameter MoE, 32B active. Strong at coding, long-context, and agentic tasks.
  • Kimi K1.5 — previous generation, still available, good at reasoning tasks.
  • Kimi k2.6 — updated K2 variant with improved instruction following and research capabilities.

Free tier

Free tier available at kimi.ai with daily usage limits. No credit card required. Paid plans unlock higher limits and API access.

Strengths

  • 1M token context window
  • Excellent for very long documents
  • Comprehensive, ranked responses
  • Strong at coding and agentic tasks
  • Includes keyboard/mouse bundle awareness in shopping tasks

Limitations

  • Chinese company — data sovereignty considerations
  • Can miss brief constraints (GPU spec, budget floor)
  • Occasional factual errors on hardware comparisons
  • Interface less polished than Claude or ChatGPT
💡

When Kimi is the right tool

Kimi's 1M context window makes it the strongest option for tasks that involve very long source material — reading an entire book, analysing a large codebase, or processing a lengthy legal or financial document. For shopping research and comparisons, it returns well-ranked, thorough outputs — but always verify GPU and RAM specs against your original brief, as it may under-shoot hardware requirements.

GLM (Zhipu AI)

🔬

GLM — ChatGLM

bigmodel.cn/chat · Zhipu AI · Free tier available

GLM (General Language Model) is the flagship model series from Zhipu AI, a Chinese AI research company spun out of Tsinghua University. ChatGLM is the chat interface built on top of this model family and is one of the most capable Chinese-language AI assistants available, with strong bilingual (Chinese/English) performance.

The GLM-4 generation (including GLM-4.7 and GLM-4V for multimodal tasks) is well-suited for structured research, product analysis, and technical explanations. It tends to think carefully about caveats before answering — when official specs aren't finalized, for example, GLM will say so explicitly rather than guessing silently.

Model lineup

  • GLM-4.7 — current flagship text model, strong at research and structured outputs.
  • GLM-4V — multimodal variant, accepts image and text inputs.
  • GLM-4 Air / Flash — faster, lighter variants for everyday tasks.
  • CogVideoX / CogView — Zhipu's image and video generation models, accessible via the same platform.

Free tier

Free access available at bigmodel.cn/chat (international). The Chinese interface is at chatglm.cn. A free Zhipu account is required. API access uses a credit system — new accounts receive free credits on sign-up.

Strengths

  • Strong bilingual Chinese/English
  • Explicit about uncertainty and caveats
  • Multimodal via GLM-4V
  • Structured, practical buyer advice
  • Tsinghua University research lineage

Limitations

  • Chinese company — data sovereignty considerations
  • International interface less polished than Chinese version
  • May miss brief-specific GPU/RAM floors on shopping tasks
  • Less established in Western markets than Claude or ChatGPT
💡

When GLM is the right tool

GLM is a strong choice for bilingual workflows, Chinese-language tasks, or when you want an AI that is upfront about what it doesn't know. Its tendency to flag caveats (like unfinished official specs) before diving into recommendations makes it more reliable for research where precision matters. For creative writing and casual conversation, Claude or ChatGPT will feel more natural.

Pi (Inflection AI)

π

Pi

pi.ai · Inflection AI · Free

Pi is Inflection AI's personal AI assistant, designed from the ground up to be a supportive, empathetic conversational companion — not a research engine. Where most AI tools are optimised for tasks, Pi is optimised for people. It's designed to listen, ask follow-up questions, help you think through problems, and offer emotional support. Think of it as a life coach or a thoughtful friend, not a search engine with a chat interface.

This makes Pi genuinely excellent for its intended use case — working through a difficult decision, journaling-style reflection, explaining something confusing in plain language, or just having a supportive conversation. It's less well-suited to research tasks that require accurate spec-checking, multi-retailer comparisons, or precise hardware recommendations.

Key characteristics

  • Model: Inflection-3 Pi — built specifically for conversational, empathetic interaction.
  • Voice mode: Pi has a high-quality voice interface, one of the best among AI assistants for natural spoken conversation.
  • Memory: Pi remembers context across conversations over time, building a picture of you and your goals.
  • Web search: Available but limited — Pi may attempt product searches but can surface inaccurate specs or misidentify retailer availability.

Free tier

Pi is free at pi.ai. No credit card required. Available on web and mobile apps.

Not the right tool for technical research

Pi's web search is not designed for rigorous product research. This isn't a flaw in Pi — it's a tool being used outside its intended purpose. Use Pi for what it's built for: conversation, reflection, and support.

Strengths

  • Empathetic, supportive tone
  • Long-term memory across conversations
  • Great for thinking through decisions
  • Free, no credit card

Limitations

  • Not suited for technical research or spec-checking
  • Web search can surface inaccurate product data
  • May miss multi-retailer requirements
  • Less capable at structured task execution

Use Pi when you want to talk something through rather than look something up. Career decisions, relationship questions, building a habit, processing a difficult situation, or just wanting someone to listen without judgment. Its voice mode is especially good — it's one of the most natural AI voice experiences available. For research, shopping, coding, or fact-checking, use a different tool and come back to Pi when you want to think through what you found.

Leo AI (Brave Browser)

brave.com/leo · Built into Brave browser · Free with Brave

Leo is Brave's built-in AI assistant, accessible directly from the browser sidebar. Unlike standalone AI tools, Leo is deeply integrated with the browser — it can read and summarise the current web page, help you understand content as you browse, and answer questions in context. It's the only AI in this guide that lives inside your browser rather than as a separate tab.

Leo runs multiple model options through a single interface. The default free model rotates, and Brave Leo Premium unlocks access to more powerful models. The Qwen VL variant adds multimodal capability — the ability to analyse images and visual content alongside text.

Available models

  • Llama 3.1 8B — default free model, fast and general-purpose.
  • Mixtral 8x7B — available on free tier, better for complex tasks.
  • Claude 3 Haiku — available via Leo Premium, faster Claude model.
  • Qwen VL — multimodal variant; accepts images and visual content alongside text prompts. Useful for analysing screenshots, product images, or visual data.

Key capability: reading the current page

Leo's most distinctive feature is that it can read the webpage you currently have open. Ask it to summarise the article you're reading, extract key information from a product page, compare specs across two tabs, or answer questions about what's on screen. This is different from web search — Leo reads your current browser context, not the wider internet.

Privacy model

Conversations with Leo are not stored or used for training. Brave does not sell data to advertisers. Consistent with Brave's core privacy-first browser philosophy.

Free tier

Leo is free with the Brave browser. No account required for basic use. Leo Premium (~$15/month) unlocks higher-tier models including Claude variants.

Strengths

  • Reads current browser page
  • Multimodal via Qwen VL (image + text)
  • Privacy-first, no conversation storage
  • Multiple model options in one sidebar
  • No separate account needed
  • Honest about browsing limitations

Limitations

  • Cannot browse live retailer inventory in real-time
  • Best models require Leo Premium subscription
  • Requires Brave browser (not standalone)
  • Default free model less capable than Claude/GPT-4o
💡

When Leo is the right tool

Leo is at its best when used while browsing — summarising a long article, extracting specs from a product page, asking questions about what you're reading, or comparing information across tabs. For research tasks that require browsing live retailer inventory, Leo will tell you honestly that it can't do that in real-time. Use it with Qwen VL to analyse product images or screenshots alongside your query.

Free vs. paid — what's actually different?

The free tiers of major AI tools are genuinely useful — don't let anyone tell you otherwise. For most everyday tasks, they're more than enough. Here's an honest breakdown of what you actually get when you pay.

🔢

More messages per day

Free tiers limit how many times you can prompt per day or per hour. Heavy users will hit these limits. Paid tiers remove or significantly raise the cap. If you only use AI occasionally, free is fine.

🧠

Access to more powerful models

The "best" model from each company (Claude Opus, GPT-4o, Gemini 1.5 Pro) is usually gated behind a paid plan. The free models are still good, but the paid models are noticeably better for complex tasks, long documents, and subtle writing work.

📄

Larger context windows

Paid plans let you feed longer documents, longer conversations, and more context to the AI. If you're working with long reports, books, or code files, this matters. For short questions and everyday tasks, it usually doesn't.

🌐

Web browsing and integrations

Real-time web access, image generation, file uploads, and third-party plugins are often paid-tier features — though Gemini's web access is available free. Copilot has web access free through the Edge browser.

💡

Our honest take on paying

If you're using AI daily for work — writing, analysis, coding, research — $20/month for a paid plan pays for itself almost immediately in time saved. If you're experimenting or using it occasionally, stick to the free tiers until you hit a limit that frustrates you. That frustration is your cue to upgrade.
